## What Is VA Disability Compensation?
VA disability compensation is a tax-free monthly payment provided to veterans who were injured, became ill, or had existing conditions worsened during military service.

Under the VA Disability Rates, payments are based on:
- Your disability rating (0% to 100%)
- Severity of your condition
- Number of dependents (spouse, children, parents)

The higher your rating, the higher your monthly compensation.

## How to Increase Your VA Disability Rating
If your condition has worsened, you may qualify for a higher rating—and higher monthly payments under the VA Disability Rates.

**Steps to Increase Your Rating**
- Submit VA Form 21-526EZ
- Provide updated medical evidence
- Include a nexus letter linking your condition to service
- Add buddy statements to support your claim

Many veterans leave money on the table simply because their evidence isn’t strong enough. A properly documented claim can significantly improve your outcome.

## How to Go from 80% to 100% VA Disability
Reaching a 100% disability rating is life-changing—but it requires strong proof.

To move from 80% to 100%, you must show:
- Your conditions have worsened significantly
- Your combined rating meets VA criteria
- OR you qualify for Total Disability based on Individual Unemployability (TDIU) if you can’t maintain employment

Accurate documentation and medical support are critical here.

## How to File a VA Disability Claim (Step-by-Step)
Starting your claim the right way can impact how much back pay you receive.

**Basic Process**
1. File an Intent to File (VA Form 21-0966)
2. Gather medical records and service evidence
3. Submit your claim using VA Form 21-526EZ
4. Attend required VA exams
5. Wait for your rating decision

A small mistake at any stage can delay or reduce your benefits.

## Sleep Apnea VA Claim: What You Need to Know
Sleep apnea is one of the most commonly claimed conditions—and often denied due to weak evidence.

To file successfully, you need:
- A confirmed diagnosis through a sleep study
- Proof it began during service, or a secondary connection
- A strong medical nexus letter

Without these, your claim may not hold up—ensure you have proper medical evidence.
