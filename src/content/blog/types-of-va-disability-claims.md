Knowing the different claim types is essential to choosing the right path and getting proper VA disability claim support.

## Direct Service Connection Claim
This is the most common claim type.

Your condition is directly caused by military service
- **Example:** Knee injury during training

✔ **Requires:**
- Medical diagnosis
- Service records showing injury
- Nexus linking both

## Secondary Service Connection Claim
A condition caused by an existing service-connected disability.

- **Example:** Depression caused by chronic pain
- **Example:** Heart disease due to hypertension

✔ Strong evidence is critical here
✔ Often increases total compensation

## Presumptive Service Connection Claim
The VA assumes certain conditions are service-related based on your service history.

Common cases include:
- Agent Orange exposure
- Burn pit exposure
- Gulf War illnesses

✔ No need to prove direct connection
✔ Faster approvals when done correctly

## Aggravation Claim
If you had a condition before service that worsened during service:

- **Example:** Pre-existing asthma becoming severe

✔ Must prove the condition worsened due to service
✔ Requires medical comparison (before vs after)

## Increased Disability Claim
If your condition has worsened over time:

- You can request a higher disability rating
- Leads to increased monthly compensation

✔ Requires updated medical reports
✔ Must show worsening symptoms

## Supplemental Claim
Filed when your claim was denied but you now have new evidence.

- **Example:** New medical diagnosis or test results

✔ Must include new and relevant evidence
✔ One of the most effective ways to overturn denials

## Special Claims
For unique or additional benefits such as:

- Total Disability Individual Unemployability (TDIU)
- Temporary 100% disability
- Special Monthly Compensation (SMC)
- Clothing allowance or hospitalization benefits

✔ Often overlooked but highly valuable

## When Should You File a VA Claim?
Timing matters when it comes to VA disability claim support.

You can file:
- Before discharge (within 180 days)
- Immediately after service
- Years later (no time limit for most claims)

💡 **The earlier you file, the sooner you receive compensation.**

## How VA Disability Ratings Work
The VA assigns a rating from 0% to 100% based on severity.

- Higher rating = higher monthly payment

**Ratings are based on:**
- Medical evidence
- Severity of symptoms
- Impact on daily life and work

✔ Even a 0% rating can qualify you for healthcare benefits

## Common Conditions for VA Disability Claims
Veterans commonly file claims for:

- PTSD and mental health conditions
- Back pain and musculoskeletal injuries
- Hearing loss and tinnitus
- Sleep apnea
- Migraines
- Diabetes
- Hypertension
- Exposure-related illnesses (burn pits, chemicals)

Each condition requires strong documentation and proper filing strategy.

## Why Veterans Get Denied (And How to Avoid It)
Many claims are denied due to:

- Lack of medical evidence
- Missing nexus (service connection)
- Incomplete documentation
- Poor C&P exam preparation

With proper support for VA disability claims, these issues can be avoided.
